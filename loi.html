Letter of Intent — EV Charging Pilot

Preliminary Terms Only · Only Section 3 Is Binding
Date —
Provider Plumeria Lux LLC, Operating as BasepointGrid
Property —
Property Manager —
This letter covers the basic terms for an EV charging pilot at this property. It is not the full contract — that comes later as a Site-Host Agreement. Only Section 3 below is binding once signed.
1

The Pilot

We install one dual-port Level 2 charger, two parking spots, at $0 cost to you. We handle billing and resident support. You get 10% of what the charger earns, growing up to 15% as the property's charging capacity grows — more stalls, panel upgrades, that kind of thing. We'll spell out the exact growth steps in the full agreement.

2

Getting It Built

Once you sign this, and your resident survey shows enough interest, we start the engineering work and apply through PG&E's Rule 29 program to help cover install costs. Once we have the engineering plan, you'll line up your own electrician for the conduit run.

3

If the Deal Falls Through

If we've already paid for engineering and PG&E approval, and Property cancels or switches to another vendor within 12 months, Property owes us back what we spent — capped at $3,750 total (PG&E's advance, up to $3,500, plus a $250 admin fee). If the project turns out technically unworkable, this letter just ends. Nobody owes anybody anything.

★ This section is legally binding upon signature
4

Not a Full Contract

Outside Section 3, nothing here is binding. This is just us agreeing to move forward together.

5

Signing Authority

By signing, you're confirming you have the authority to do this for the property.
