New apartment buildings in the East Bay now offer EV charging as a standard feature. Without it, you risk losing good tenants to buildings that have it.
3
It may protect you legally
California law requires you to approve tenants who want to install their own chargers — which means random electrical work and lost power capacity. Properties with shared EV charging may be exempt from individual tenant charger requests under California Civil Code §1947.6. Ask your property attorney if this applies to you.

Common Questions
What about our assigned parking spots?
We only use visitor or voluntarily swapped spaces. Not one assigned resident spot is touched without their agreement. EV-driving tenants choose to swap — everyone else keeps their spot.
What if we sell the property?
The site license transfers to the new owner — it becomes a selling point, not a liability. Documented monthly EV charging income strengthens your NOI and asking price.
What happens after 5 years?
You can renew, renegotiate, or we remove the equipment at our cost. Your property is never stuck with dead hardware. ChargePoint stations typically run 10–15 years.
